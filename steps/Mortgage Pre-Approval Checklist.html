WHY GET PRE-APPROVED?

Getting pre-approved for a mortgage is a crucial first step in your home buying journey. Pre-approval:

  • Shows sellers you're a serious and qualified buyer
  • Gives you a clear picture of your budget and price range
  • Identifies potential credit issues early in the process
  • Streamlines the buying process once you find a home
  • Strengthens your negotiating position in competitive markets

In today's competitive Tucson market, having a pre-approval letter is essential before beginning your home search.

BASIC DOCUMENTS (REQUIRED BY ALL LENDERS)

Identification

  • Valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport)
  • Social Security card or number
  • For non-U.S. citizens: Green card or visa documentation

Income Verification

  • W-2 forms from all employers for the past two years
  • Pay stubs from the last 30 days showing year-to-date income
  • Federal tax returns (all schedules) for the past two years
  • If self-employed or earning significant income from commissions or bonuses:
  • Business tax returns for the past two years (if applicable)
  • Profit and loss statement (year-to-date)
  • 1099 forms for the past two years

Assets Documentation

  • Bank statements for checking and savings accounts (last 2 months, all pages)
  • Investment account statements (last 2 months, all pages)
    • - Retirement accounts (401(k), IRA, pension)
    • - Brokerage accounts
    • - Certificates of deposit
  • Documentation for any other significant assets
  • Source documentation for any large deposits
  • Gift letter (if receiving down payment assistance from a family member)

Debt Information

  • List of all current debts and obligations with:
    • - Creditor names
    • - Account numbers
    • - Monthly payment amounts
    • - Current balances
    • - Payoff amounts (if paying off any debts before closing)
  • Explanation letter for any recent credit inquiries

Residence History

  • Addresses for the past two years with:
    • - Dates of residency
    • - Monthly rent or mortgage payment amounts
    • - Landlord contact information (if renting)
  • If currently own a home:
    • - Mortgage statement showing current balance and payment
    • - Homeowners insurance policy
    • - Property tax statements

ADDITIONAL DOCUMENTS (MAY BE REQUIRED)

For VA Loans

  • Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
  • DD-214 for veterans
  • Statement of service for active duty

For Self-Employed Borrowers

  • Business license
  • Letter from CPA verifying business existence and your income
  • Business bank statements (last 2-3 months)
  • Year-to-date profit and loss statement

For Other Income Sources

  • Divorce decree/separation agreement (if receiving or paying alimony/child support)
  • Documentation for Social Security, disability, or pension income
  • Rental property income documentation (tax returns, current leases)

For Non-Traditional Credit

  • Proof of on-time payments not typically reported to credit bureaus:
    • - Utility bills
    • - Rent payments
    • - Insurance payments
    • - Cell phone bills

Special Situations

  • Bankruptcy discharge paperwork (if applicable)
  • Foreclosure, short sale, or loan modification documentation (if applicable)
  • Explanation letter for any credit issues or special circumstances
  • Documentation supporting source of down payment

DOCUMENTS NEEDED AFTER FINDING A HOME

  • Fully executed purchase contract
  • Earnest money deposit receipt
  • Contact information for homeowners insurance agent
  • HOA information (if applicable)
  • Home inspection report (when completed)
  • Condominium or PUD questionnaire (if applicable)

TIPS FOR A SMOOTH PRE-APPROVAL PROCESS

  1. Organize documents in advance
    Create digital copies of all documents and organize them in clearly labeled folders.
  2. Respond promptly to lender requests
    Additional documentation may be requested during the process. Quick responses keep things moving.
  3. Avoid major financial changes
    During the pre-approval and home buying process, avoid:
    • Opening new credit accounts
    • Making large purchases
    • Changing jobs
    • Making large deposits without documentation
    • Closing credit accounts
    • Co-signing loans for others
  4. Check your credit report
    Review your credit report from all three bureaus before applying and address any errors.
  5. Maintain consistency
    Use the same name format on all applications and documents.
  6. Compare multiple lenders
    Shop around with 3-4 lenders within a 14-day period for the best rates without multiple hits to your credit score.

UNDERSTANDING PRE-QUALIFICATION VS. PRE-APPROVAL

Pre-qualification:
  • Quick, informal review of your financial situation
  • Often based on self-reported information
  • No formal verification of documents
  • Less valuable to sellers
Pre-approval:
  • Thorough verification of financial documentation
  • Credit check and detailed analysis
  • Stronger indication of loan eligibility
  • Much more valuable in the home buying process

Always seek pre-approval rather than just pre-qualification.

ARIZONA DOWN PAYMENT ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS

Arizona offers several programs to help qualified buyers with down payment and closing costs:

1. Home Plus Program
  • Up to 5% of loan amount for down payment/closing costs
  • Income limits apply (varies by county)
  • Must complete homebuyer education course
2. Pathway to Purchase (P2P)
  • Available in specific Arizona cities including parts of Tucson
  • Down payment assistance up to 10% of purchase price
  • Must be owner-occupied primary residence
  • Income restrictions apply
3. Pima Tucson Homebuyer's Solution
  • Specific to Pima County and Tucson residents
  • Down payment assistance options
  • Income and purchase price limits apply

Additional documentation will be required when applying for these programs. Ask your lender for specific requirements.
