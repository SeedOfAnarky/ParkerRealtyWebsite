Step 3: Home Search & Viewings

The home search is often the most exciting part of the buying process. With our Home Touring Agreement in place, we'll start exploring properties that match your criteria. I'll help you evaluate homes objectively and provide market insights to inform your decisions.

Finding the right home involves a combination of market knowledge and understanding your unique needs.

How We'll Find Your Perfect Home

Custom Property Matching

I'll set up a personalized search in our Multiple Listing Service (MLS) based on your criteria. You'll receive notifications when new properties that match your preferences hit the market.

Pre-Screening Properties

To save you time, I'll preview properties when possible to ensure they meet your basic requirements before scheduling a viewing.

I'll help you keep track of properties with detailed notes, photos (when permitted), and a comparison sheet to reference when making decisions.

Tucson Neighborhood Guide

Location Matters

In real estate, location is paramount. Understanding Tucson's diverse neighborhoods will help you find not just a great house, but the right community for your lifestyle.

Central Tucson/University Area

Character: Historic homes, vibrant atmosphere

Price Range: $350K-$700K

Highlights: Close to University of Arizona, restaurants, cultural venues

Considerations: Older homes may need updates, student population

Catalina Foothills

Character: Upscale, scenic mountain views

Price Range: $500K-$2M+

Highlights: Luxury homes, excellent schools, hiking trails

Considerations: Higher price point, further from downtown

Oro Valley

Character: Family-friendly, master-planned communities

Price Range: $400K-$900K

Highlights: Top-rated schools, golf courses, newer homes

Considerations: Commute to downtown, HOA restrictions

East Tucson/Tanque Verde

Character: Spacious lots, desert landscape

Price Range: $400K-$800K

Highlights: More land, mountain views, equestrian properties

Considerations: Further from city amenities, rural feel

What to Look for During Viewings

When touring homes, focus on these key areas to make informed comparisons:

Feature What to Consider
Location & Lot Neighborhood amenities, proximity to work/schools, lot size, privacy, views, noise levels
Layout & Space Floor plan functionality, room sizes, storage space, future flexibility for your changing needs
Systems & Age Age of major components (roof, HVAC, water heater), energy efficiency features, potential maintenance needs
Natural Light & Orientation Window placement, direction the home faces (important in Arizona for energy efficiency)
Outdoor Space Landscaping, maintenance requirements, outdoor living areas, potential for additions

Remember that cosmetic issues (paint colors, fixtures, etc.) can be easily changed, while structural elements, location, and lot characteristics are permanent.

Parker's Pro Tips

Take Photos with Permission

With the seller's permission (which I'll secure), take photos of each property during viewings. This helps you remember specific features when comparing homes later. I recommend creating a dedicated album for each property, including photos of any areas that might need attention or renovation.

Create a Consistent Rating System

Develop a simple 1-5 rating system for the key features that matter most to you (location, layout, condition, etc.). Rate each property immediately after viewing while your impressions are fresh. This objective approach helps prevent being swayed by a single stunning feature while overlooking important drawbacks.
