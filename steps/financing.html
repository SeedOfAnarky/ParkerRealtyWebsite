Step 6: Loan Processing & Appraisal

While the home inspection process is taking place, your mortgage lender will be hard at work processing your loan application and ordering an appraisal. This phase typically runs concurrently with the inspection period but often extends beyond it, usually taking 30-45 days in total to complete.

The loan approval process involves multiple steps and requires your active participation to stay on track.

The Mortgage Approval Process

Loan Application Submission

Your lender will require you to complete a full application and submit supporting documentation. This is more comprehensive than the pre-approval process.

Document Review

The lender's underwriting team will review your financial documents, credit history, employment verification, and other required information.

Property Appraisal

The lender will order an appraisal to verify that the property value supports the loan amount. This is a crucial step in the process.

Underwriting

The underwriter evaluates the complete package (your finances and the property) to determine if the loan meets the lender's requirements and federal guidelines.

Conditional Approval

The lender may issue a conditional approval, requiring additional documentation or explanations for specific items before final approval.

Clear to Close

Once all conditions are satisfied, the lender issues a "clear to close," allowing you to proceed to closing.

Respond Promptly to Requests

Your lender will likely ask for additional documents or clarifications during the process. Responding quickly helps keep your closing on schedule. Even a day's delay in providing requested information can potentially push back your closing date.

Understanding the Appraisal Process

The appraisal is a critical step in the mortgage process, as it verifies that the property is worth at least the amount you're paying:

What Is an Appraisal?

An appraisal is an independent evaluation of a property's value conducted by a licensed appraiser. The appraiser will visit the property, take measurements and photos, and compare it to similar recently sold properties in the area.

Cost: $450-$600 in the Tucson market

Timeline: Typically 5-10 business days after ordering

Potential Appraisal Outcomes

At or Above Purchase Price: The loan proceeds as planned.

Below Purchase Price: This creates an "appraisal gap" that must be addressed through one of these options:

  • Renegotiate the purchase price with the seller
  • Pay the difference out of pocket
  • Request a reconsideration of value (if appropriate)
  • Cancel the contract (if your appraisal contingency is still in effect)

Handling Appraisal Issues

Gather Evidence

If the appraisal comes in low, we'll review the report for errors or omissions, such as incorrect square footage or missing features.

Find Better Comps

We can search for comparable sales that better match your property and may justify a higher value.

Request Reconsideration

If we find substantial evidence, your lender can request a reconsideration of value from the appraiser.

Negotiation with Seller

If the appraisal stands, I'll help negotiate with the seller to reduce the price to match the appraised value.

Evaluate Options

If neither reconsideration nor negotiation works, we'll discuss your options for making up the difference or potentially walking away.

Appraisal Gap Coverage

In competitive markets, some buyers include "appraisal gap coverage" in their initial offer. This states that you'll pay a certain amount (up to a specified limit) if the property appraises below the purchase price. While this strengthens your offer, only include it if you have the financial means to cover the potential gap.

Parker's Pro Tips

Avoid Major Financial Changes

During the loan process, avoid making significant financial changes that could impact your approval. Don't apply for new credit, make large purchases, change jobs, or make unusual bank deposits or withdrawals without consulting your lender first. Even seemingly positive changes like paying off a car loan can affect your debt-to-income ratio calculations.

Document Everything

Keep detailed records of all communications with your lender and maintain copies of everything you submit. If you're sending sensitive financial documents via email, consider using a secure service or password-protected files. Create a dedicated folder for your mortgage process to stay organized as requests come in.
