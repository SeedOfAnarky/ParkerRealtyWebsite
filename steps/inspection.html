Step 5: Due Diligence Period

Once your offer is accepted, we enter the due diligence periodâ€”a critical time when you'll investigate the property thoroughly before fully committing to the purchase. In Arizona, this typically lasts 10 days and provides essential protections for you as a buyer.

Time-Sensitive Process

The due diligence period has strict deadlines. We'll need to act quickly to schedule inspections, review reports, and submit any repair requests within the timeframe specified in your contract (typically 10 days in Arizona).

Professional inspections reveal important details about the property's condition that aren't visible during typical showings.

The Home Inspection Process

Scheduling the Inspection

I'll recommend reputable inspectors and help coordinate scheduling, ideally within 2-3 days of offer acceptance to leave time for additional specialist inspections if needed.

Inspection Day

A general home inspection typically takes 2-4 hours. I recommend attending if possible, as this gives you the opportunity to ask questions and see issues firsthand.

Report Review

You'll receive a detailed report with photos documenting the property's condition and any issues found. We'll review it together to identify significant concerns.

Repair Request Preparation

Based on the inspection findings, we'll draft a Buyer's Inspection Notice and Seller's Response (BINSR) listing items you want the seller to address.

Negotiation Period

After receiving your repair request, the seller typically has 3-5 days to respond. They may agree to all repairs, some repairs, offer a credit, or decline to make any repairs.

Decision Point

Based on the seller's response, you'll decide whether to proceed with the purchase, negotiate further, or cancel the contract and receive your earnest money back.

Buyer Inspection Costs

In Arizona, the buyer typically pays for inspections, which are non-refundable regardless of the results or whether you proceed with the purchase. Consider these upfront costs as an investment in your peace of mind and protection from potentially expensive surprises later.

For a typical single-family home, budget $500-$800 for a general inspection plus termite inspection. Additional specialized inspections will increase this amount.

Common Issues in Arizona Homes

Based on my experience with Tucson properties, here are common inspection findings to be aware of:

Roof Issues

Arizona's intense sun and monsoon season can damage roofing materials. Cracked tiles, deteriorated shingles, and improper flashing are common findings.

HVAC Concerns

Air conditioning systems work hard in our climate and may show wear or improper maintenance. Older units may have efficiency or refrigerant issues.

Water Damage

Despite our desert climate, water damage from plumbing leaks, roof leaks, or improper drainage around the foundation is surprisingly common.

Electrical Issues

Older homes may have outdated electrical panels, ungrounded outlets, or amateur modifications that don't meet current code requirements.

Pest Activity

Termites, scorpions, and roof rats are common in Arizona. Evidence of current or past infestations requires careful evaluation.

Drainage Problems

Poor grading around the foundation can lead to water intrusion during monsoon season, potentially causing structural issues.

Creating Effective Repair Requests

Not all inspection findings merit repair requests. Here's how to prioritize:

Focus on Significant Issues

Prioritize safety concerns, structural problems, and major system defects (roof, HVAC, electrical, plumbing). Minor cosmetic issues are typically not worth including.

Be Specific

Clearly describe each issue and specify how you want it addressed (repair, replacement, credit). Vague requests lead to misunderstandings and potential disagreements.

Reference the Inspection Report

Include page numbers or specific sections from the inspection report to provide documentation of the issues.

Consider Market Conditions

In competitive seller's markets, aggressive repair requests may jeopardize the deal. In buyer's markets, you can be more assertive.

Present Multiple Options

When appropriate, give sellers choices: fix the issue, provide a credit at closing, or reduce the sale price. This flexibility often leads to successful negotiations.

Parker's Pro Tips

Attend the Inspection

While not required, I strongly recommend attending at least the last hour of your home inspection. The inspector can show you issues directly and explain their significance in a way that photographs in a report can't capture. This firsthand knowledge is invaluable when making repair requests and planning for future maintenance.

Consider a Home Warranty

For homes with aging systems and appliances, consider requesting that the seller provide a home warranty as part of your repair negotiations. These plans typically cost $400-$600 for one year and can provide coverage for unexpected breakdowns after closing. We can include this request in your repair negotiations.
