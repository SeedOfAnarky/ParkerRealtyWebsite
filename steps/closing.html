Step 8: Closing Day

Congratulations! You've reached the final step in your home buying journey. Closing day (also called settlement day) is when you'll sign the final paperwork, pay closing costs, and officially take ownership of your new home. In Arizona, closings typically take place at a title company office, with all parties signing documents either together or separately.

Closing day involves signing numerous documents that finalize your home purchase and mortgage.

Before Closing Day

Review Closing Disclosure

At least three business days before closing, your lender will provide a Closing Disclosure detailing your final loan terms and closing costs. Review this document carefully and compare it to your Loan Estimate to identify any unexpected changes.

Prepare Funds

Arrange for the transfer of required funds. In Arizona, you'll typically need a cashier's check or wire transfer for amounts over $10,000. Personal checks are generally not accepted for large sums.

Schedule Utilities

Contact utility companies to schedule service transfers into your name effective on closing day or your planned move-in date.

Conduct Final Walkthrough

As discussed in Step 7, perform a final walkthrough 24-48 hours before closing to ensure the property is in the expected condition.

Gather Required Documents

Prepare your photo ID, proof of insurance, and any other documents requested by your lender or title company.

Wire Fraud Warning

Be extremely cautious about wire transfer instructions. Real estate transactions are targets for wire fraud. Always verify wire instructions by calling your escrow officer directly using a phone number you've independently confirmed. Never trust wire instructions sent via email without verbal confirmation.

What to Bring to Closing

Essential Items

  • Photo ID: Government-issued identification (driver's license, passport)
  • Funds: Cashier's check or wire transfer confirmation for closing costs
  • Proof of Insurance: Documentation of your homeowners insurance policy
  • Closing Disclosure: Your copy with any questions noted

Additional Helpful Items

  • Purchase Contract: Your copy for reference
  • Contact Information: Phone numbers for your lender, agent, and attorney
  • Personal Checkbook: For any minor adjustments to closing costs
  • Calculator: To verify figures if needed

Understanding Closing Costs

Closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the home's purchase price. Here's a breakdown of common closing costs in Arizona:

Fee Type Typical Cost Description
Loan Origination Fee 0.5-1% of loan amount Charged by the lender for processing your loan application
Discount Points 0-3% of loan amount Optional fee paid to lower your interest rate (1 point = 1% of loan amount)
Title Insurance $700-$2,000+ Protects your ownership rights and lender's investment
Escrow Fees $500-$1,000 Paid to the title company for handling the closing process
Appraisal Fee $450-$600 Cost of the property appraisal (often paid before closing)
Recording Fees $75-$200 Government fees for recording the new deed and mortgage
Prepaid Expenses Varies widely Property taxes, homeowners insurance, and mortgage interest paid in advance

Escrow/Impound Accounts

Many lenders require escrow accounts for managing ongoing property taxes and insurance. At closing, you'll need to fund this account with several months of these expenses in advance:

  • Property Taxes: Typically 2-6 months of taxes
  • Homeowners Insurance: Often a full year's premium plus 2 months

These aren't additional costs (you'd pay these expenses regardless), but they do increase your upfront cash needs at closing.

What Happens at Closing

Arrive and Present ID

You'll arrive at the title company office, present your identification, and meet with the escrow officer who will guide you through the process.

Review and Sign Documents

You'll sign numerous documents (typically 50-100 pages), including the promissory note, mortgage/deed of trust, and various disclosures. The escrow officer will explain each document.

Pay Closing Costs

You'll provide your cashier's check or confirm that your wire transfer has been received for the closing costs and down payment.

Funding and Recording

The lender will fund the loan, and the title company will record the deed and mortgage with the county. This may happen immediately or take a few hours.

Receive Keys

Once the loan is funded and documents are recorded, you'll receive the keys to your new home. In Arizona, this typically happens on the same day as signing.

The entire closing process usually takes 1-2 hours for the signing portion, with recording and funding potentially taking another few hours.

Key Documents You'll Sign

Promissory Note

Your legal promise to repay the loan according to the stated terms, including interest rate, payment amount, and length of loan.

Deed of Trust/Mortgage

Secures the loan by giving the lender a lien on your property. This is the document that allows foreclosure if you default.

Closing Disclosure

Details the final loan terms, monthly payments, and itemized closing costs. You'll compare this to the estimate you received earlier.

Deed

Transfers legal ownership of the property from the seller to you.

After Closing: Your First Steps as a Homeowner

Change the Locks

For security, rekey or replace all exterior locks as soon as possible after closing. You don't know who might have copies of the existing keys.

Set Up Utilities and Services

Confirm all utilities are in your name, including water, electricity, gas, internet, and trash service.

Update Your Address

File a change of address with the post office and update your address with banks, employers, subscription services, and government agencies.

Store Important Documents

Keep your closing documents in a safe, accessible location. You'll need them for taxes, insurance claims, and future refinancing.

Parker's Pro Tips

Schedule a Morning Closing

When possible, schedule your closing for early in the morning. This provides buffer time for resolving any unexpected issues that arise and increases the likelihood that you'll receive keys the same day. Afternoon closings can be delayed until the next business day if there are complications or if wire transfers arrive too late for same-day processing.

Take Time Off Work

Plan to take the full day off work for closing, even if you expect it to take only a couple of hours. This removes the pressure of rushing through document reviews and gives you time to address any last-minute issues. It also allows you to begin moving or making immediate changes to your new home once you receive the keys.
