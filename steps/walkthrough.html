Step 7: Final Walkthrough

The final walkthrough is your last opportunity to verify the property's condition before closing. It typically takes place 24-48 hours before closing and allows you to confirm that the home is in the expected condition, agreed-upon repairs have been completed, and no new issues have emerged since your last viewing.

The final walkthrough gives you a chance to verify the property's condition just before you take ownership.

Critical Last Step

While it may seem like a formality, the final walkthrough is a crucial protection. Once you sign closing documents, addressing issues becomes much more complicated and potentially expensive. Take the time to be thorough during this final inspection.

Final Walkthrough Goals

Verify Repairs

Confirm that all agreed-upon repairs from the inspection period have been completed properly. Ask for receipts or documentation of professional work if available.

Check for New Issues

Inspect for any new damage or issues that may have occurred since your previous visits, such as water damage, HVAC problems, or other changes.

Test Systems and Appliances

Turn on and test all systems, including plumbing, electrical, HVAC, appliances, and fixtures to ensure everything is in working order.

Confirm Inclusions

Verify that all items included in the purchase contract are present and in the expected condition (appliances, window treatments, etc.).

Check for Seller's Belongings

Ensure the seller has removed all personal belongings and trash (unless specific items were agreed to remain).

Comprehensive Walkthrough Checklist

I recommend systematically checking these items during your final walkthrough:

Exterior

  • Roof and gutters visually appear in same condition as during inspection
  • Landscaping is maintained and irrigation system works (if applicable)
  • Exterior walls show no new damage
  • Garage door opener functions and all remotes are provided
  • Pool/spa equipment operates properly (if applicable)
  • Outdoor lighting fixtures work

Interior: General

  • No new damage to walls, floors, or ceilings
  • Windows and screens are intact and functional
  • Doors open, close, and lock properly
  • Light fixtures and ceiling fans work
  • Smoke and carbon monoxide detectors function
  • No signs of new pests or rodents

Kitchen

  • All appliances included in sale are present and working
  • Faucets and sink operate with no leaks
  • Garbage disposal functions
  • Cabinet doors and drawers open properly
  • Countertops are free of new damage

Bathrooms

  • Toilets flush properly and don't run or leak
  • Sinks, showers, and tubs drain properly
  • No leaks under sinks or around fixtures
  • Exhaust fans operate correctly
  • No evidence of new water damage

Systems

  • HVAC system turns on and produces heat/cool air
  • Water heater is operational
  • Circuit breaker panel is accessible and labeled
  • All electrical outlets work
  • Water main shutoff is accessible

Documentation

  • Repair receipts/warranties for completed work
  • Manuals for appliances and systems
  • Garage door openers and programming instructions
  • Keys to all exterior doors
  • Alarm codes and instructions (if applicable)
  • HOA documents and parking permits (if applicable)

Bring These Items to Your Walkthrough

  • Cell phone: For taking photos, using as a flashlight, and testing outlets
  • Original inspection report: To verify repairs and compare conditions
  • Copy of repair requests/agreements: To confirm all agreed work was completed
  • Measuring tape: To confirm appliances will fit or measure for furniture
  • Outlet tester: Simple and inexpensive way to check electrical outlets
  • Notebook and pen: To document any concerns or questions

Handling Walkthrough Issues

If you discover problems during the final walkthrough, we have several options:

Issue Severity Potential Solutions
Minor Issues
(Light bulbs out, minor cleaning needed)
  • Proceed with closing; address issues yourself
  • Request a small credit (often handled at closing)
Moderate Issues
(Appliance not working, incomplete agreed-upon repairs)
  • Negotiate a repair credit at closing
  • Escrow funds for repairs to be completed later
  • Delay closing until issues are resolved
Major Issues
(New water damage, missing fixtures, significant new problems)
  • Delay closing until resolved
  • Substantial credit or price reduction
  • In extreme cases, cancel the contract (consult with a real estate attorney)

If issues arise, I'll help you negotiate with the seller to find an acceptable solution. In most cases, sellers want to close on time and are willing to work with reasonable requests to address legitimate concerns.

Parker's Pro Tips

Document with Photos

Take detailed photos during your final walkthrough, especially of completed repairs and overall property condition. These can be invaluable if any dispute arises after closing. Create a systematic approach, photographing each room from multiple angles and paying special attention to areas where repairs were made.

Check Utilities Status

Verify that all utilities are still on and functioning during the walkthrough. In some cases, sellers may have scheduled disconnection close to the closing date. Confirm that electricity, water, gas, and internet services are active so you can properly test all systems. This also prevents the inconvenience of having to reestablish services after closing.
