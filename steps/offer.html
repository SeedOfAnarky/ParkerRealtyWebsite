Step 4: Offer & Earnest Money

When you've found the perfect home, the next step is submitting a compelling offer. This is where my expertise in Tucson's market and negotiation strategy becomes particularly valuable. I'll help you craft an offer that protects your interests while being attractive to sellers.

A well-crafted offer balances competitive terms with necessary protections for the buyer.

Anatomy of a Strong Offer

A competitive offer in today's market includes more than just the price. Here are the key components we'll discuss:

Component Why It Matters
Purchase Price Based on comparable sales, market conditions, and the property's condition. I'll help you determine a competitive yet fair price.
Earnest Money Deposit Shows your commitment to the purchase. In Tucson, typically 1-2% of the purchase price, held in escrow.
Financing Terms Details about your loan, including down payment amount and pre-approval status. Stronger offers have solid financing in place.
Contingencies Conditions that must be met for the sale to proceed, such as inspection, appraisal, and financing. These protect you but can weaken offers if excessive.
Closing Timeline When you'll complete the purchase. Can be strategic based on seller needs (30-45 days is typical in Arizona).
Included Items Specifies which appliances, fixtures, or other items are included in the sale.

Understanding Earnest Money

Important Earnest Money Facts

Earnest money is a "good faith" deposit that demonstrates your commitment to the purchase. It becomes part of your down payment at closing but is at risk if you default on the contract.

Amount

In the Tucson market, earnest money typically ranges from 1-2% of the purchase price. For a $400,000 home, that's $4,000-$8,000. The amount can be adjusted based on market competition.

Submission Timeline

You'll need to submit earnest money within 1-3 business days after offer acceptance. The escrow company will provide wire transfer or check instructions.

Protection

Contingencies in the contract (inspection, appraisal, financing) protect your earnest money. If you need to back out for a reason covered by these contingencies, you'll receive your deposit back.

Potential Risk

If you back out of the purchase for reasons not covered by contingencies, you could forfeit your earnest money to the seller. This is why proper contingencies are crucial.

Final Disposition

At closing, your earnest money is applied to your down payment and closing costs. It's not an additional expense but rather an advance payment.

Essential Contingencies

Contingencies protect you by allowing you to back out of the contract under specific circumstances. Here are the most important ones to include:

Inspection Contingency

Gives you the right to have the property professionally inspected and to negotiate repairs or cancel the contract based on findings.

Timeframe in Arizona: Typically 10 days

Importance: Critical for discovering hidden issues

Financing Contingency

Protects you if your mortgage application is denied despite good faith efforts to secure financing.

Timeframe in Arizona: Usually 30-45 days

Importance: Essential unless paying cash

Appraisal Contingency

Allows you to renegotiate or cancel if the property appraises for less than the offer price.

Timeframe in Arizona: Typically within 30 days

Importance: Protects you from overpaying

Home Sale Contingency

Makes your purchase contingent on selling your current home first.

Timeframe in Arizona: Varies, often 30-60 days

Importance: Optional but helpful if you need to sell first

Note: Can weaken offers in competitive markets

Negotiation Strategy

This is where my expertise as your agent truly shines. I'll help you craft a winning offer by:

  • Researching the seller's motivations (timeline, reason for selling, etc.)
  • Analyzing comparable sales to justify our offer price
  • Structuring contingencies to protect you while remaining competitive
  • Including personal touches (like a buyer letter in appropriate situations) to stand out
  • Negotiating for the seller to cover my commission, honoring my $1 promise to you

Remember: The best offers balance competitiveness with protection.

The Offer Submission Process

Offer Preparation

We'll complete all required paperwork, including the purchase contract (typically 10+ pages in Arizona) and supporting documents.

Review and Signatures

I'll explain each document thoroughly before you sign. Most signatures are completed electronically for convenience.

Submission to Seller

I'll present your offer to the seller's agent, highlighting its strengths and your qualifications as a buyer.

Response Window

In our offer, we'll set a deadline for the seller's response (typically 24-48 hours).

Possible Outcomes

The seller may accept, reject, or counter your offer. If countered, we'll discuss whether to accept their terms, counter back, or walk away.

Acceptance and Earnest Money

Once all parties sign, you'll deposit earnest money with the escrow company within the specified timeframe (typically 1-3 business days).

Parker's Pro Tips

Have Your Pre-Approval Updated

Before submitting an offer, contact your lender to get a property-specific pre-approval letter. This shows sellers you're serious and financially capable of completing the purchase. Many sellers won't consider offers without current pre-approval.

Consider an Escalation Clause in Competitive Situations

In multiple-offer scenarios, an escalation clause automatically increases your offer to a specific amount above the highest competing offer, up to your maximum. This can be a powerful strategy in hot markets, allowing you to stay competitive without overpaying.
