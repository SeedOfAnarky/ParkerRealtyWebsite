Step 2: Home Touring Agreement

Following the recent National Association of Realtors (NAR) settlement, a Home Touring Agreement is now required before viewing properties. This document protects both you and me by clearly defining our professional relationship and establishing transparency about compensation.

Important Update

As of May 2024, the NAR settlement requires written agreements between buyers and their agents before touring homes. This is a nationwide change designed to increase transparency in real estate transactions.

Understanding the Home Touring Agreement

The Home Touring Agreement is specifically designed for the property viewing stage of your home buying journey. It's more limited in scope than a full Buyer Representation Agreement, focusing specifically on establishing our professional relationship for showing properties.

Here's what the agreement covers:

A sample Home Touring Agreement with key sections highlighted.

Key Components of the Agreement

Section What It Means
Agent Responsibilities Outlines what I will do for you as your agent, including showing properties, providing market information, and protecting your confidential information.
Compensation Structure Details how I'll be compensated, including my $1 promise if I can't get the seller to cover my commission. This section ensures complete transparency about potential costs.
Term of Agreement Specifies how long the agreement will last. For Home Touring Agreements, this is typically 30-90 days, giving you plenty of time to explore the market without a long-term commitment.
Termination Provisions Explains how either party can end the agreement if needed. I believe in pressure-free relationships, so my agreements include reasonable termination options.
Fair Housing Commitment Affirms my commitment to fair housing laws and non-discrimination in all real estate practices.

Home Touring vs. Full Buyer Representation

It's important to understand the difference between the initial Home Touring Agreement and a comprehensive Buyer Representation Agreement:

Home Touring Agreement Full Buyer Representation Agreement
Limited to property viewing stage Covers the entire purchasing process
Shorter term (typically 30-90 days) Longer term (often 3-6 months)
Focused on showing properties Includes offer preparation, negotiations, and closing
Initial step in our working relationship Established after you decide to move forward with a purchase

If you decide to proceed with making an offer on a property, we'll transition to a full Buyer Representation Agreement at that time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is signing this agreement legally binding?

Yes, it is a legally binding document that establishes our professional relationship. However, it's specifically limited to the home touring process and includes clear termination provisions if you wish to end our professional relationship.

Can I work with multiple agents under different home touring agreements?

While legally possible, it's generally not recommended. The agreement is designed to establish a relationship where I'm working in your best interests. Working with multiple agents simultaneously can create conflicts and confusion. Instead, I encourage you to interview several agents first, then choose one you trust.

What happens if I want to cancel the agreement?

The agreement includes a termination clause that allows either party to end the relationship with written notice. I believe in no-pressure relationships - if we're not a good fit, I want you to find an agent who better meets your needs.

Can the terms of the agreement be modified?

Yes, the terms are negotiable. During our initial consultation, we'll review the agreement together, and I'm happy to discuss any adjustments that would make you more comfortable with the arrangement.
